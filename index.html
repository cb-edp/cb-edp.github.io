Why do we have an email development system? Needs a rewrite, copied it

Consistency It gives us well defined documentation so everyone who touches emails at Treehouse will be on the same page. A consistent email design language which aligns with our brand allows our users to become immersed in the full Treehouse experience.

Efficiency This guide takes the guesswork out of email design to make it an easier process. With the templates and reusable components, you no longer have to reinvent the wheel as you embark on creating an email. Common patterns are already defined, and all you have to do is replace the content. Also, there's no more fighting with inline styles, as the CSS is contained in a central place. And to top it all off, you're able to start with a stable foundation that has already been thoroughly tested.

How is it organized?

The email development system provides guidelines and tools for the visual creation of our emails. You will not find any of our regularly maintained code here, but instead will find visual representations of our emails and the components they use.

  • Information about what you need to know when building emails in HTML, like basic frameworks and rules to adhere to.
  • Components that we use to create our emails in both our marketing ESP and in-house software.
    • Frameworks are used to define our basics in email development. A set of CSS rules and a basic template to put our elements into.
    • Header contains all elements related to the header section of the email. Pieces like the blue logo bar and USP's at the top of an email.
    • Body containing all content elements in a mail. Product positions, leaderboards, blog items and so on.
    • Footer is in regards to all the elements we use to close off an email. Close Alert, the footer and more.
  • Templates that show you the kind of emails that are already being sent and how you can use and combine different components that are already available.
  • Tools to help you get started with email development, to try new combinations of components and to see how they look together without even having to code anything.
